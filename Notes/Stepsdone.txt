# First set of filtering that we do on dataset after the audit

There are two sets of filtering that we can do on the data
1. Structural validity filters - if something is not supposed to be there, 
   it should not be there types, hard filters, like year being out of desired years
   - Data quality gates retained ~96% of 2023–2024 records but only ~90% of 2025, reflecting both the project’s Jan–Nov 2025 coverage 
   boundary and elevated anomaly rates in 2025 raw records.

- On further deep dive into the dropped rows, 
found that vendor 2, payment_type =0, RatecodeID = null and store_and_fwd_flag = null has a major problem
- The spread check across months show that the bad rows are almost evenly spread out, so it is not month ingestion issue

2. Behavior/ Outlier filters
   - clipping max fare to be 300, min fare to be 2.5
   - max duration to be 240, etc

# Hyperparameter tuning
I ran a controlled hyperparameter study rather than a blind search. 
Shallower trees underfit the problem, while increasing max_depth to 10 improved future-holdout MAE. 
Lower learning rate with more trees was competitive but not best on MAE, and stronger subsampling/regularization did not improve
 temporal generalization in this setup
